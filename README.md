# Leave Analysis – Excel Project

## Business Problem

Managing leave records manually in an organization can be challenging. Without proper analysis, it is difficult to track leave trends, identify employees or departments with high leave frequency, and plan resources efficiently. For the Provost Squadron, understanding leave patterns is essential to ensure operational readiness while maintaining employee satisfaction.

This project analyzes leave data to answer key business questions such as:

- Which employees or departments take the most leave?  
- What is the distribution of leave types (annual, casual, sick)?  
- Are there seasonal or day-of-week trends in leave?  
- Which periods experience the highest concentration of leave requests?  

The goal is to transform raw leave records into actionable insights for better decision-making and squadron management.

---

## Project Objectives

- Summarize total leaves taken by each employee and department  
- Categorize leave types and analyze their frequency  
- Identify patterns in leave by day, week, and month  
- Detect anomalies or excessive leave patterns  
- Provide actionable recommendations to improve planning and policy enforcement  

---

## Tools & Techniques Used

- **Microsoft Excel** – main tool for analysis  
- **Pivot Tables** – to summarize leave counts and trends  
- **Conditional Formatting** – highlight patterns and anomalies  
- **Charts & Graphs** – visualize leave distributions and trends  
- **Formulas & Functions** – SUMIF, COUNTIF, AVERAGEIF for calculations  

---
## Business Impact

- Helps the squadron plan staffing more efficiently and avoid understaffing.  
- Enables data-driven decisions for leave approvals and policy adjustments.  
- Supports identification of employees or departments that may need better workload management.  
- Turns raw leave data into actionable insights for operational readiness.

## Key Insights

- Certain employees consistently take leave on Mondays and Fridays, indicating potential long weekends.  
- Specific departments show higher leave frequency, which may impact operational readiness.  
- Casual and annual leave dominate, while sick leave occurs mostly during specific months.  
- Peak leave periods occur during national holidays or seasonal breaks.  

---

## Recommendations

- Implement scheduling adjustments to cover high-leave periods.  
- Encourage employees to distribute leave evenly across the year.  
- Review leave policies for departments with consistently high leave frequency.  
- Use these insights for workforce planning and performance management.
